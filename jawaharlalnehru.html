Jawaharlal Nehru

About Jawaharlal Nehru

Jawaharlal Nehru (1889–1964) was a key leader of the Indian independence movement and served as the **first Prime Minister of independent India** from 1947 until his death. He is widely considered the architect of the modern Indian nation-state.

A staunch believer in democracy, socialism, and secularism, Nehru laid the foundation for India's domestic and foreign policies, introducing the concepts of the **Five-Year Plans** and the **Non-Aligned Movement (NAM)** on the global stage.

Known affectionately as **'Pandit Nehru,'** he was deeply committed to promoting science, technology, and universal education, shaping India into a stable, democratic republic in its early years.

Story

Privilege and Education

Jawaharlal Nehru was born on **November 14, 1889**, in Allahabad. He was the son of Motilal Nehru, a wealthy barrister and prominent nationalist leader. Nehru received a privileged education, attending prestigious institutions in England, including **Harrow School** and **Trinity College, Cambridge**. He returned to India in 1912, intending to practice law, but soon found himself drawn into the burgeoning struggle for independence.

The Call to the Freedom Struggle

Nehru’s life changed profoundly after meeting **Mahatma Gandhi** in 1916. Inspired by Gandhi’s call for mass mobilization and non-cooperation, Nehru abandoned his Western lifestyle and legal career to fully dedicate himself to the cause of freedom. He quickly rose through the ranks of the **Indian National Congress**, suffering imprisonment multiple times at the hands of the British authorities for his activism.

A Vision for Independent India

Nehru was a radical voice within the Congress, advocating for complete independence (**Purna Swaraj**), which was officially adopted as the goal of the Congress in 1929 under his presidency. His vision for a free India was clear: it would be a **secular, democratic republic** built on socialist principles, ensuring equality for all citizens. His intellectual depth and modern outlook made him the spiritual successor to Gandhi in leading the nation forward.

The Midnight Hour (1947)

On **August 15, 1947**, at the stroke of midnight, Jawaharlal Nehru delivered his iconic **'Tryst with Destiny'** speech, marking India's independence. As the first Prime Minister, his primary task was nation-building—integrating hundreds of princely states, establishing a parliamentary democracy, and modernizing a poor, newly independent nation. He prioritized science, industry, and a planned economy.

Architect of Foreign Policy

On the international stage, Nehru was the pioneer of the **Non-Aligned Movement (NAM)**, positioning India as a leader among newly independent nations that wished to remain outside the Cold War power blocs. His focus on peace, international cooperation, and anti-colonialism earned him respect as a global statesman. He remained Prime Minister until his death in 1964, leaving behind a legacy as the creator of modern India's democratic foundations.

Famous Quotes

  • “A moment comes, which comes but rarely in history, when we step out from the old to the new.” (From the 'Tryst with Destiny' speech)
  • “Peace is not merely the absence of war, but a state of mind.”
  • “The children of today will make the India of tomorrow.”
  • “Failure comes only when we forget our ideals and objectives and principles.”
