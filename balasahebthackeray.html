Balasaheb Thackeray

About Balasaheb Thackeray

Bal Keshav Thackeray (1926–2012) was a charismatic and controversial **Indian politician, cartoonist, and founder of the Shiv Sena**, a Hindu nationalist and Marathi regional party. He was often referred to as the **'Hindu Hriday Samrat'** (Emperor of Hindu Hearts).

Initially a cartoonist with *The Free Press Journal*, he used his artistic skills to critique the government and champion the cause of **Marathi pride and Marathi-speaking people** in Mumbai.

Though he never held any formal government post, his political influence over Maharashtra, especially Mumbai, was immense, making him one of the most powerful and influential figures in modern Indian politics.

Story

From Pen to Power: The Cartoonist's Beginning

Balasaheb Thackeray was born on **January 23, 1926**, in Pune, Maharashtra. His father, Keshav Sitaram Thackeray (Prabodhankar Thackeray), was a prominent social reformer and writer. Balasaheb began his professional life as a **cartoonist** at *The Free Press Journal* in Mumbai. In 1960, he left to start his own political weekly, **'Marmik,'** which used powerful cartoons and satire to criticize the growing non-Marathi influence in Mumbai and advocate for the rights of the **'Marathi Manoos'** (Marathi people).

The Birth of Shiv Sena (1966)

In **1966**, Balasaheb founded the **Shiv Sena** party. Named after the legendary Maratha warrior Chhatrapati Shivaji, the party’s initial platform was strongly regionalist, demanding preferential treatment for Maharashtrians in jobs and education in Mumbai. The movement quickly gained traction among the working class and youth, using mass mobilization tactics and a fiery regionalist rhetoric to establish a strong political foothold in the city.

Shifting Ideology and Political Clout

By the 1980s, the Shiv Sena's ideology shifted from purely regional issues to a more robust platform of **Hindutva** (Hindu nationalism). Thackeray became an outspoken proponent of this cause, broadening the party's appeal across Maharashtra. His powerful oratory skills, delivered through large, charismatic rallies, allowed him to command the loyalty of millions. He successfully transformed the Shiv Sena from a city-based regional party into a significant force in state and national politics.

The Remote Control of Power

Despite his immense influence, Balasaheb Thackeray famously chose **never to contest an election** or hold any constitutional office. He preferred to govern from outside, famously calling himself the **'Remote Control'** that guided his party's governments. The Shiv Sena first came to power in Maharashtra in coalition with the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in **1995**, a historic moment that showcased his unparalleled influence.

A Legacy of Influence

Balasaheb Thackeray passed away on **November 17, 2012**. His passing brought the city of Mumbai to a complete standstill, marking the depth of respect and influence he commanded. His legacy is one of a leader who built a political movement based on fierce cultural identity and left an indelible mark on the political landscape of Maharashtra and India.
