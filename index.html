Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Iron Man of India, senior leader of the Indian National Congress, and independent India's first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister.

Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj — the indomitable Tiger of Deccan, architect of Hindavi Swarajya, and ruler of justice, unity, and cultural pride.

Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar is a legendary Indian cricketer regarded as the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket.

Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose - Leader of the Indian National Army, known for his slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

B. R. Ambedkar

He was one of the most influential leaders in modern Indian history.Architect of the Indian Constitution and a crusader for social justice.

MILKHA SING

He was the ‘Flying Sikh’, a legendary sprinter A symbol of speed and determination, he inspired generations with his remarkable journey from struggle to glory.

CHHATRAPATI SAMBHAJI MAHARAJ

Sambhaji Maharaj was a fearless Maratha warrior who defended Swaraj with unmatched courage. His determination and sacrifice made him a symbol of bravery and resilience in Indian history.

MAHATMA GANDHI

Mahatma Gandhi was the leader of India’s freedom movement and a symbol of truth and non-violence. His peaceful struggle inspired millions and changed the course of history.

SWAMI VIVEKANANDA

Swami Vivekananda was a visionary spiritual leader who introduced India’s philosophy to the world. His teachings inspired youth to pursue strength, knowledge, and self-belief.

SAWATRI BAI PHULE

Savitribai Phule was India’s first female teacher and a pioneer of women’s education. Her courage and activism opened the doors of learning and equality for millions.

BALASAHEB THACKERAY

Balasaheb Thackeray was a prominent Indian political leader and founder of the Shiv Sena. Known for his strong oratory and influence in Maharashtra, he shaped regional politics for decades.

MAHARANA PRATAP

Maharana Pratap was a legendary Rajput warrior known for his unwavering courage and devotion to freedom. His bravery in the Battle of Haldighati made him an eternal symbol of valor in Indian history.

JAWAHARLAL NEHRU

Jawaharlal Nehru was independent India’s first Prime Minister and a key architect of modern India. He championed democracy, scientific progress, and a vision of a progressive nation.

CAPTAIN VIKRAM BATRA

Captain Vikram Batra was a brave Indian Army officer who became a Kargil War hero through his fearless leadership. His battle cry “Yeh Dil Maange More” continues to inspire the nation’s spirit of courage and sacrifice.
