Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

About Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630 - 1680) was a great Maratha warrior and the founder of the **Maratha Empire** in western India. Born in the hill-fort of **Shivneri**, he rose to prominence with his **guerrilla warfare** tactics and valor. King of dhackhan.

He established a progressive civil rule with well-structured administration. Shivaji Maharaj was known for his **respect for all religions**, courage, and visionary leadership. He was crowned as **Chhatrapati in 1674 at Raigad Fort**, and his legacy remains a symbol of pride and bravery in Indian history.

Story

A Brave Baby is Born – 1630

Long ago, in the Sahyadri mountains of Maharashtra, there was a strong stone fort called **Shivneri**. **(A Brave Baby is Born – 1630)** Inside this fort, on **19 February 1630**, a baby boy was born to Shahaji Raje Bhosale and **Jijabai**. Before his birth, Jijabai had prayed to Goddess Bhavani for a son who would protect the people. Holding the newborn, she whispered: “You will never bow to injustice, my Shivba.” From childhood, Shivaji liked playing with toy swords and bows. He enjoyed riding horses and exploring the hills, preparing unknowingly for the life of a warrior.

The Big Dream – 1645

Shivaji spent most of his early life at Pune, where Jijabai raised him with strong values. **(The Big Dream – 1645)** She told him stories of Rama, Krishna, and the brave kings of the past. One day, when he was around 15, he stood on a hill with his friends and looked at the land below. It was rich and beautiful — but ruled by foreign kings like the Mughals and the Adil Shah of Bijapur. Shivaji said to his friends: “Our land should belong to our own people. We will build a **Swarajya** — a kingdom of freedom!” His friends, the **Mavalas**, promised to help him no matter what. That day, the dream of Swarajya began.

The First Fort is Ours – 1646

Shivaji’s first target was **Torna Fort**, a huge fort in the mountains. With only a small group of loyal Mavalas, he climbed the steep paths at night. The guards at the fort were not expecting trouble, so the young warriors were able to take control without much fighting. By morning, the saffron flag was flying proudly on Torna. Shivaji smiled and said: “This is our first step. Soon, many more forts will join us.” This victory made other villages believe in his dream.

The Brave Mavalas and More Forts

Shivaji’s soldiers were called **Mavalas** because they came from the Maval region near Pune. They were farmers and hunters, used to living in the mountains. They could run up steep slopes, hide in forests, and fight with great skill. With their help, Shivaji captured many forts one after another — Purandar, Rajgad, Kondhana (later called **Sinhagad**), and more. Each fort became a safe place for his people and a strong defense against enemies. By now, the saffron flag could be seen from one hill to the next.

The Night Attack on Shaista Khan – 1663

The Mughal emperor Aurangzeb sent **Shaista Khan**, a powerful general, to defeat Shivaji. Shaista Khan captured Pune and moved into **Lal Mahal**, which was Shivaji’s own childhood palace! Shivaji could not bear this insult. He decided on a bold plan. One night, he and 400 Mavalas disguised themselves as wedding guests and quietly entered Pune. Moving through dark alleys, they reached Lal Mahal. With a sudden burst, Shivaji entered Shaista Khan’s bedroom and attacked him. Shaista Khan escaped, but not before losing three fingers to Shivaji’s sword. By the time the Mughal soldiers reacted, Shivaji and his men had disappeared into the night. It was one of the most daring raids in Indian history.

The Trick of the Tiger Claws – 1659

Even before Shaista Khan’s attack, the Bijapur Sultan had sent his strongest general, **Afzal Khan**, to crush Shivaji. Afzal Khan was huge and powerful, and he tried to lure Shivaji into a trap by inviting him for a private meeting at the base of **Pratapgad Fort**. Shivaji suspected danger. He wore armor under his clothes and hid a deadly weapon — the **wagh nakh** (tiger claws) — in his hand. When Afzal Khan tried to stab him during the meeting, Shivaji struck back with the tiger claws. Afzal Khan fell, wounded, and the Maratha army, waiting nearby, charged into battle. By the end of the day, the Marathas had won a huge victory.

The Great Escape from Agra – 1666

Aurangzeb later invited Shivaji to **Agra** for peace talks. But instead of giving him respect, Aurangzeb placed him behind lesser nobles in the court. Shivaji felt insulted and walked out. The Mughals placed him under house arrest. Shivaji planned a clever escape. He pretended to send baskets of sweets to the poor. Inside two of those baskets were **Shivaji and his son Sambhaji**! The baskets were carried out of Agra without suspicion, and by the time anyone noticed, Shivaji was far away.

The Chhatrapati of the People – 1674

After many victories, Shivaji was crowned king at **Raigad Fort in 1674**. The fort was decorated with flowers and saffron flags. Priests from across the land came to bless him. Shivaji became **Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj**. He ruled with fairness — protecting farmers, respecting women, and giving importance to all religions. He also built a strong navy to guard the coastline.

The Last Sunset – 1680

* On **3 April 1680**, at Raigad Fort, Shivaji Maharaj passed away. But his dream of Swarajya did not die with him. His ideals inspired generations of freedom fighters in India. Even today, people remember him as the brave king who fought for justice, freedom, and the dignity of his people. BUt we Can feel him in the Sahyadri.
