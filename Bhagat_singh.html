Bhagat Singh

About Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh (1907–1931) was one of India’s most influential freedom fighters. Born in Punjab, he became a symbol of bravery and revolutionary spirit against British rule. At a very young age, he joined the fight for independence and became a voice of the youth.

He was involved in the Central Legislative Assembly bombing in 1929, intended as a protest, not to harm anyone. Bhagat Singh embraced death fearlessly and was executed at the age of 23. His sacrifice continues to inspire millions across India.

Legacy of a Legend

Even today, the name Bhagat Singh stands for bravery, sacrifice, and deep love for the motherland. He proved that even a short life, lived with courage and purpose, can inspire generations to come.
