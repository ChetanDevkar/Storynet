Sachin Tendulkar

About Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar (born 1973) is regarded as one of the **greatest cricketers of all time**. Known as the **"Master Blaster"** and **"God of Cricket,"** he inspired millions with his passion, dedication, and humility.

He made his debut for India in 1989 at the age of 16 and went on to become the **highest run-scorer in international cricket history**. Tendulkar was the first cricketer to score **100 international centuries** and the first to score a double century in ODIs.

He received the **Bharat Ratna**, India's highest civilian award, and is celebrated not just for his records, but for his sportsmanship and contribution to Indian cricket.

Story

A Young Prodigy is Found

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar was born on **April 24, 1973**, in Mumbai. His father, Ramesh Tendulkar, was a renowned Marathi novelist. As a mischievous young boy, he was sent to cricket coach **Ramakant Achrekar** at the age of 11. Achrekar quickly recognized Sachin's extraordinary talent and dedication. Sachin would practice for hours, and his coach would often place a rupee coin on the stumps; if Sachin remained 'not out,' he won the coin. This simple act taught him focus and discipline.

The Record-Breaking Partnership

In 1988, at the age of 14, Sachin and his schoolmate Vinod Kambli set a world-record partnership of **664 runs** in a school match. This feat brought him into the national spotlight and fast-tracked his career. Just a year later, at the tender age of **16**, he made his debut for the Indian cricket team against Pakistan, facing legendary fast bowlers like Waqar Younis and Wasim Akram.

The Little Master's Reign

Sachin's career spanned 24 years, during which he broke almost every major batting record. His dedication transcended mere statistics; he carried the burden of a billion expectations on his shoulders in every match. Key milestones include scoring his first Test century in England at the age of 17, and his legendary "Desert Storm" innings in Sharjah in 1998, which captivated the world.

The Ultimate Dream

Despite years of individual success, the one trophy that eluded him was the Cricket World Cup. Finally, in **2011**, playing in his sixth World Cup, India lifted the trophy in his home city of Mumbai. This victory was a heartfelt tribute from his teammates, who carried him on their shoulders around the Wankhede Stadium—a moment that remains an iconic symbol of his dedication to the nation.

Legacy and Retirement

Sachin retired from all forms of international cricket in **November 2013**, after playing his 200th Test match. He finished his career as the leading run-scorer and century-maker in both Test and ODI cricket. Beyond the records, his legacy is one of humility, respect for the game, and inspiring generations across India and the world to pursue their passion with dedication.

Famous Quotes

  • “People throw stones at you and you convert them into milestones.”
  • “I have never tried to compare myself to anyone else.”
  • “Chase your dreams but make sure you don’t find shortcuts.”
