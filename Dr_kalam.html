Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015), known as the **"Missile Man of India"**, was a brilliant scientist and the **11th President of India**. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family and worked hard to achieve his dreams.

He played a key role in India’s space and missile development programs, including the **Pokhran-II nuclear tests**. Beyond science, he inspired millions of students with his words and vision. He believed in dreaming big and working hard.

He passed away while addressing students in 2015, doing what he loved most – sharing knowledge. He is remembered as a visionary, a teacher, and a true patriot.

Story

A Humble Beginning in Rameswaram

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on **October 15, 1931**, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a homemaker. His family was financially poor, and young Kalam had to **sell newspapers** after school to help support the family. This early life taught him the value of hard work and education.

The Flight That Inspired a Scientist

Kalam was an average student but had a deep curiosity for learning, especially about science and flight. A moment that changed his life forever was when his teacher, Sivasubramania Iyer, took the class to the seashore and taught them how a bird flies. This inspired him to pursue aeronautical engineering at the **Madras Institute of Technology (MIT)**.

The Missile Man of India

After graduating, Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was the project director for India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (**SLV-III**), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit. His greatest contribution was leading the development of Indigenous Guided Missiles (like **Prithvi** and **Agni**), earning him the title, **"Missile Man of India."**

The People's President

In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his term, he became known as the **"People's President"** for his approachable nature and his deep connection with the youth. After leaving office in 2007, he returned to his true passion: teaching and writing. He believed that the youth were the greatest assets of the nation.

A Legacy of Inspiration

Dr. Kalam passed away on **July 27, 2015**, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He literally died while doing what he loved—inspiring students. His vision for India, laid out in his book **‘India 2020’**, continues to guide the nation. He remains one of India’s most revered scientists and leaders.
